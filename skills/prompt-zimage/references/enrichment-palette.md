# Enrichment Palette — Z-Image

Open this file whenever you're in **Enhance Mode** (user gave a thin seed prompt or asked you to improve a draft). It's the menu you pick from. Pick by scene-type, not by working down the list — most prompts use 4–6 enrichments total, drawn from 3–4 of the categories below.

## The off-center detail rule (master rule)

Every Enhance Mode output MUST contain exactly ONE small, slightly unexpected, hyper-specific detail. This is the single biggest anti-generic move — without it, even a richly enriched prompt regresses toward stock-photo mean.

Examples:

- A band-aid on the left knuckle
- A coffee ring on the upper-left of the manuscript
- A half-finished crossword on the table, ballpoint laid across it
- A small chip in the front tooth visible only when she smiles
- A faded ink stamp on the back of one hand from last night's bar
- One sleeve rolled up higher than the other
- A bobby pin tucked behind one ear, holding nothing in place
- An open paperback with the spine creased flat at page 47
- A loose thread at the cuff she keeps absent-mindedly worrying
- A receipt face-up on the table showing a single line item
- One shoelace tied tighter than the other
- A faded marker line on the back of one hand from a hand-stamp

The detail should feel **observed**, not invented. If you can imagine a documentary photographer noticing it across the room, it belongs. If it reads as a prompt-engineering trick, drop it.

**One per prompt. Never zero. Never more than one.** Two off-center details cancel each other out (the eye reads them as styling).

## Scene type → which categories to lean on

| Scene type | Lean heavily on |
|---|---|
| Single-subject portrait | Subject specifics, body mods, accessories, wardrobe, named-reference anchor, bias-guard sub |
| Character in environment | Subject specifics, wardrobe, environmental props, light interaction, bias-guard sub |
| Environment / architecture | Environmental props, light interaction, weather, era/mood anchor, one human trace |
| Product / object | Material specificity, light interaction, surface staging, named-reference anchor |
| Poster / flat-lay artifact | Material specificity (paper/ink), page-level imperfections, font/text specificity, era anchor |
| Action / sports | Subject specifics (muscle, sweat, motion), wardrobe condition, environmental motion, off-center mishap detail |
| Retro / synthwave / neon-noir | Light interaction (neon-noir cluster), wardrobe (80s era), named-reference anchor (synthwave cluster), off-center detail |
| Bilingual editorial / Chinese-aesthetic | Wardrobe (era), light interaction, named-reference anchor (Wong Kar-wai / Shanghai), bilingual text |
| Abstract / surreal | **Skip the rubric.** Let the seed lead. Add light + one off-center detail + one named anchor only. |

## 1. Subject specifics (face / skin / hair / hands)

Use 1–3 on any human subject. AI defaults to symmetrical and unblemished; specificity here is the difference between "doll" and "person."

**Face:** faint asymmetry (one eye slightly smaller, slight cant to the jaw); a chipped front tooth visible when smiling; a small gap between the front teeth; a faint scar through one brow / on the chin / across the philtrum; a beauty mark below the lip; smile lines around the eyes; a slight overbite; a sunburn line where sunglasses sat; one earlobe slightly larger than the other.

**Skin:** freckles across the bridge of the nose / scattered down the shoulders / sun-spots on the cheekbones; visible pores on the cheekbones in close-up; a faint sheen of sweat at the hairline / collarbone / upper lip; a sun-tan line at the sleeve / collar / sock; a healing scrape on the knee / elbow / shin; an ink stain along the side of the writing-hand pinky; faded summer henna on the back of one hand; dirt under one or two fingernails; a small pimple on the jaw / forehead (not a focal point).

**Hair:** a flyaway strand drifting across the eye; tucked behind one ear, the other left loose; a single bobby pin holding nothing in place; slept-on, faintly disheveled; recently cut, slightly uneven at the edges; wet, still drying; a single grey hair visible; roots starting to show under dye; a piece of lint or leaf debris caught in it.

**Hands:** calloused palms (specify cause: guitar strings, rope, weights); a ring-finger callus from a removed ring; paint- / ink- / clay-stained; a band-aid on one knuckle; a faint tan line from a removed watch / bracelet; dirt under specific nails; chipped nail polish (specify color); a faded ink stamp on the back of one hand; a scab on one finger from a recent cut.

## 2. Body modifications

High-leverage non-generic move. AI underweights these because stock imagery suppresses them. **AVOID "covered in tattoos" / "lots of piercings" — be specific about content and placement.**

**Tattoos** (place + content; keep them small unless the prompt asks otherwise):
- A small hand-poked bee on the inner left wrist
- A line of small cursive along the right ribcage
- Three small stars along the inside of the right index finger
- A faded blackwork floral half-sleeve from elbow to shoulder on one arm
- A simple line drawing of a hand on the back of one calf
- A single small heart inside the lower lip (only visible when smiling)
- Behind one ear: a tiny crescent moon
- A faded ankle band of dots
- A stick-and-poke smiley on the thumb-pad
- A linework snake curling around the right bicep

**Piercings:**
- Two stacked helix studs on the right ear (and one lobe, gold)
- A small septum hoop, silver, tucked up most of the time
- A nostril stud, faint, gold
- A single eyebrow stud
- Small-gauge earlobes (4–6mm — not stretched)
- A tongue piercing visible only when she speaks
- An industrial bar across the top of the right ear
- A belly-button ring barely visible above a low waistband
- A small lip ring on the lower right

**Scars / marks:**
- A thin pale scar through the left eyebrow
- A small crescent scar on the chin
- A faint surgical scar on one knee
- A long thin scar along the inside of one forearm
- A faded burn on the back of one hand (small, palm-sized)

## 3. Accessories

**Earrings:** small gold hoops; a single pearl drop on one ear, the other empty; mismatched studs; no earrings but a faint piercing scar; small silver crosses; long fringed silver dangles.

**Rings:** a thin gold signet on the right pinky; a worn wedding band; a turquoise stone in silver on the right index; a delicate snake ring around the middle finger; multiple thin stacking bands; a faint indentation from a removed ring.

**Wrists:** a vintage Timex with a sweat-darkened leather strap; a faded G-Shock scuffed at the face; a thin gold chain bracelet; a friendship bracelet in faded thread; a medical-ID bracelet; an elastic hair tie around the wrist; a wrist tan-line from a removed watch.

**Neckwear:** a thin gold chain with a single small charm (cross / locket / key / pearl); a leather cord with a wooden bead; a 90s satin choker; a medal tucked inside the shirt, chain visible; a bandana knotted at the throat.

**Glasses:** tortoiseshell wireframes; gold-frame aviators (70s); mid-century cat-eye (50s); small round Lennon frames; wraparound sport sunglasses pushed up on the forehead; wire-rim drugstore readers on a chain; 80s clear-frame oversized squares; mirrored aviators in the synthwave register.

**Hats:** sun-faded baseball cap (specify team or blank with sweat-stained brim); wool watch cap pulled low; wide-brimmed felt hat; bandana tied bandit-style at the back.

**Bags / pockets:** canvas tote stained at the bottom; leather satchel with patina at the corners; battered backpack with a single enamel pin; a phone protruding from a back pocket; a folded paperback in a coat pocket.

## 4. Wardrobe specificity

Almost never just "a dress" or "a shirt." At minimum: fabric + fit + condition. Add era when it helps.

**Fabric:** raw selvedge denim, washed linen, brushed wool, ribbed knit, technical nylon, faded indigo, suede, oxford cloth, slubbed silk, waxed canvas, brushed cotton flannel, vinyl, leather, mesh.

**Fit:** slightly oversized, tailored, cropped, draped, structured, slim, boxy.

**Condition:** faded, wrinkled, freshly pressed, slept-in, paint-stained, sun-bleached, mended at one shoulder, missing a top button, hem fraying, hand-darned at the elbow.

**Era anchors:** 1970s, mid-2000s, 1990s vintage, post-war utility, fin-de-siècle, Y2K, late-70s punk, early-90s grunge, mid-century menswear, 1980s power-shoulder, 1980s synthwave-adjacent (neon-piped athletic gear, oversized blazer with rolled sleeves), 1990s Hong Kong street.

**Specific items** (vague items read as "AI" — specific items read as "real"):
- Oxford-cloth button-down (collar slightly curled)
- Cream fisherman knit sweater, hand-knit-irregular
- A-line midi skirt
- Levi's 501s with frayed cuffs
- A motorcycle jacket cracked at the elbows
- Work boots scuffed at the toe
- A barn jacket with the inside corduroy worn smooth at the collar
- A waxed canvas chore coat
- A surplus army-issue field shirt
- A faded 80s band T-shirt (don't name the band unless relevant)
- High-waisted carpenter pants with one chalk mark on the thigh
- An oversized 80s pastel blazer, sleeves pushed to the elbow
- A satin scorpion-print bomber (Drive register)
- A cropped 90s qipao top in faded jade silk

## 5. Environmental props (the "lived-in" layer)

Pick 1–2 that imply the subject was here BEFORE the photo, not staged.

**On a desk / table:** a half-finished crossword with ballpoint laid across it; a coffee ring on the upper-left; a paperback with the spine creased flat at page 47; a glass with melting ice; an ashtray with a single cigarette stub; a house key on a beaded keychain; an open notebook with handwriting visible; a receipt face-up showing a single line; a houseplant slightly wilted on one side.

**In a room:** laundry on a line across one corner; a bicycle leaning against a doorway; a child's chalk drawing on the floor; a stray cat sleeping on a windowsill; a half-deflated balloon caught against the ceiling; a coat hung over a chair-back with one sleeve dragging; one picture frame slightly askew; a radio playing (visible source, implied sound).

**Outside / street:** steam rising from a manhole; a single sock on the pavement; a torn flyer half peeled from a lamppost; a puddle reflecting one neon sign; a pigeon mid-strut in the foreground; construction tape billowing in the wind; a fading chalk hopscotch grid on the sidewalk.

**Weather / atmosphere:** faint mist rising from warm pavement after rain; snow sticking to one side of a tree; heat haze rising off asphalt; falling cherry blossoms; ash from a distant fire; fog rolling in low at ankle height; a single shaft of sun breaking through cloud; wind whipping one direction (debris, hair, leaves all consistent).

## 6. Light interaction (beyond "golden hour")

Specify how the light interacts with the scene, not just its quality:

- A single practical source (table lamp, screen glow, candle, reflected neon)
- Two-source: window light + a single warm interior lamp
- Rim light ONLY from behind — silhouette with edge glow, face in shadow
- Light filtered through a curtain / a window screen / a dirty window
- Light through fabric (a sheer scarf, a thin blouse)
- Caustics — light reflected through liquid (a glass of water on the table)
- Colored bounce: red from a brake light onto a cheek, blue from a phone screen on the face, green from a fluorescent tube above
- TV as the only light in the room
- Time-of-day named precisely: 11pm fluorescent diner, 4am parking-lot sodium-vapor, 6am pre-dawn blue, late golden hour ~7pm summer

**Neon-noir cluster** (Z-Image renders this register particularly strongly):

- Hot pink and cyan signage reflecting in rain puddles on dark asphalt
- Magenta key from a single neon tube on one side of the face, cyan rim from the opposite side, the eye sockets falling into deep cool shadow
- Sodium-vapor amber from a parking-lot lamp competing with cool blue moonlight
- A warm tungsten window leaking yellow onto a rain-slick sidewalk while a cold blue dusk sky holds the upper third of the frame
- Mirrored neon — sign + its reflection + its reflection in a puddle, three sources visually
- Hong Kong tenement light: dozens of small warm interior windows stacked vertically, the street below in deep shadow

### Bilingual / Chinese-aesthetic (Z-Image is bilingual)

Z-Image's bilingual rendering pairs well with anchors that have Chinese / East-Asian visual lineage.

| Reference | Register |
|---|---|
| Wong Kar-wai (specifically Chungking Express, In the Mood for Love) | saturated neon longing, slow shutter, narrow-alley framing |
| Chinese 80s cinema (general) | grainy color stock, warm street neon, melodramatic interior light |
| Japanese 80s manga ligne claire | clean linework, flat color, urban panel composition |

**Western anchors above still apply** — Z-Image knows Hopper, Deakins, Petra Collins, Saul Leiter as well as it knows the synthwave register. These additions are *in addition to*, not replacing.

**Eras / movements:** 1970s American New Wave cinema (Taxi Driver, Dog Day Afternoon); 1990s independent cinema (early Wong Kar-wai, Linklater); Pictorialist photography (early 1900s); Dutch Golden Age (Vermeer, Rembrandt); Y2K editorial fashion; mid-century Eastman Color; 1980s NYC street photography; 1980s Tokyo neon street; Outrun / vaporwave / synthwave revival; 1990s Hong Kong second-wave cinema.

**Use one anchor. Don't stack.** "Patrick Nagel meets Roger Deakins" produces muddy hybrid output. One anchor; the rest of the prompt does the work.

## 8. Default subject substitution (Z-Image bias guard)

Z-Image's defaults on generic occupational and role tokens are unusually narrow. The model was trained on internet imagery whose stock-photo conventions get baked into the default. Without a proactive substitution, "a doctor" will reliably render as a white man in a long white coat with a stethoscope around the neck; "a programmer" as a white man in a hoodie with glasses; "a nurse" as a white woman in scrubs. This isn't a value judgment — it's a documented behavior of the model, and the rubric corrects for it.

**The rule: whenever the seed contains a generic role token without specifics, substitute proactively.** Don't ask the user. Pick from the substitution dimensions below, draw one substitution from each relevant dimension, and write it into the subject specifics. Vary across runs — don't always pick the same substitution.

### The narrow defaults to watch for

| Role token | Z-Image's narrow default |
|---|---|
| doctor / physician | white man, long white coat, stethoscope around neck, indeterminate-age |
| nurse | white woman, light-blue or pink scrubs, ponytail, clipboard |
| programmer / developer / engineer | white man, hoodie, glasses, dark room, multiple monitors |
| scientist / researcher | white man, long lab coat, safety goggles pushed up, beakers |
| teacher (K–12) | white woman, cardigan, holding a book in front of a chalkboard |
| professor (academic) | white man, tweed jacket with elbow patches, beard, wire-frame glasses |
| chef | white man, tall toque hat, double-breasted white jacket, mustache |
| firefighter | white man, full helmet, axe over shoulder, smoke behind |
| police officer | white man, full uniform, hand near belt |
| student | young East-Asian woman, textbooks pile, backpack, white shirt |
| soldier | white man, modern US-issue tactical gear |
| athlete | thin / muscular white man or woman, generic activewear |
| dancer | thin white woman, ballet leotard, mid-pirouette |
| businessman / CEO / executive | white man, navy suit, white shirt, red tie, downtown skyline window |
| fashion model | thin white woman, made-up, runway-stiff pose |
| witch | young white woman, pointed hat, black dress |
| rock star | white man, leather jacket, long hair |

### Substitution dimensions

When you substitute, draw one option from each relevant dimension. The richer the substitution, the further from the default.

**Age range** (sample broadly — Z-Image defaults toward late-20s / mid-30s):
- mid-20s, late-20s, early-30s, mid-30s, late-30s, early-40s, mid-40s, late-40s, early-50s, mid-50s, early-60s, mid-60s

**Gender** (rotate; make explicit when the default is narrow):
- woman, man, non-binary person (be specific about presentation when relevant)

**Ethnicity** (rotate explicitly — vague descriptions default white):
- Black, South Asian, Latina/Latino, Indigenous (specify nation when known: Diné/Navajo, Lakota, Inuit, Māori, etc.), Middle Eastern (specify: Lebanese, Iranian, Egyptian, etc.), East Asian (specify: Korean, Japanese, Han Chinese, Hmong, etc.), Southeast Asian (Filipino, Vietnamese, Thai, Indonesian), mixed-heritage (specify combination)

**Body type** (Z-Image defaults slim — rotate):
- slim, average, athletic, broad, plus-size, tall and lean, short and stocky, heavyset, wiry

**Wardrobe specifics that read as professional in a non-default way**:
- Surgeon in printed surgical scrub cap (e.g., navy with white anchors) + N95 dropped to the chin + clogs, not a long white coat
- Doctor wearing teal scrubs with a stethoscope tucked through the belt loop, ID badge on a lanyard with enamel pins
- Nurse in slate-grey scrubs with a smartwatch and trauma shears in the breast pocket, hair in a bun under a printed bouffant cap
- Programmer in a worn band T-shirt and Carhartts at a standing desk in a sunlit room, mechanical keyboard, no glasses
- Scientist in a Tyvek coverall holding a tablet at a benchtop, hair under a paper cap
- Chef in a faded apron over a black T-shirt, no toque, hair tied back with a bandana, kitchen towel slung over the shoulder
- Teacher in a denim jumper and Doc Martens with a paint-stained sleeve, classroom carpet behind
- Firefighter in turnout pants and a soot-stained station T-shirt (helmet off, tucked under one arm)

### Worked substitutions (use these as templates, then vary)

- "a doctor" → *"a Black woman pediatrician in her late 40s wearing teal scrubs and a stethoscope tucked through her belt loop, kid-friendly enamel pins on her badge lanyard"*
- "a nurse" → *"a Filipino man ICU nurse in his mid-30s wearing slate-grey scrubs and trauma shears in his breast pocket, a smartwatch on his left wrist, hair cropped short"*
- "a programmer" → *"a South Asian woman software engineer in her early 30s, hair in a messy bun, wearing a faded conference T-shirt and Carhartt overalls, mechanical keyboard at a standing desk in a sunlit converted-warehouse office"*
- "a scientist" → *"an Indigenous (Diné) man research biologist in his late 50s wearing a Tyvek coverall and a paper cap, holding a tablet over a benchtop, sun coming through a high greenhouse window"*
- "a teacher" → *"a Latina elementary teacher in her mid-30s wearing a denim jumper over a striped tee and Doc Martens, paint-stained left sleeve, kneeling beside a kid's reading nook"*
- "a chef" → *"a heavyset Lebanese man chef in his early 60s in a faded canvas apron over a black T-shirt, no toque, grey hair tied back with a bandana, sweat at his hairline as he leans over the pass"*
- "a firefighter" → *"a Black woman firefighter in her early 40s in turnout pants and a soot-stained station T-shirt, helmet tucked under one arm, hair in long box braids pulled back"*
- "a student" → *"a Vietnamese-American student in his late 20s, returning to school, wearing a flannel over a thermal henley and reading glasses pushed up into his hair, laptop and a thermos on the library table"*
- "a businessman" → *"an executive team of four — a Korean woman in a charcoal suit, a Nigerian man in a tan double-breasted blazer, a Brazilian woman in a knit dress, a Pakistani man in a navy turtleneck — around a conference table"*
- "a fashion model" → *"a plus-size Black model in her early 30s with a closely-shaved head, dressed in a structured cream linen suit, standing relaxed against a concrete wall"*
- "a witch" → *"a Mexican-American woman herbalist in her mid-50s, silver-streaked hair, wearing a wool cardigan over a worn linen smock, hands stained from grinding herbs"*
- "a soldier" → *"a Sikh man infantry soldier in his late 30s in field uniform, dastar (turban) under his patrol cap, mud at the knees, his rifle slung muzzle-down"*

The substitution menu is matter-of-fact, not aspirational. Pick what serves the scene. Z-Image's defaults are too narrow to leave to chance.
